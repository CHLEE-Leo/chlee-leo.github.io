Bio

My research interest is the development of controllable AI, aiming to improving the applicability of AI to the fields of both natural and social sciences. To do that, I focus on developing a generative model that produce synthetic sequences as intended based on domain-driven goals. In particular, combining NLP techniques with RL frameworks is the major topic.

What NLP techniques and RL frameworks have in common is that they model sequential decision-making processes, therefore my problem approach usually begins by generalizing real-world problems to sequential decision-making problems. The problem is then turned into a reward maximization problem (using the RL setting) to control the decision-making process according to the user-defined reward function. Finally, we aim to propose a model in which the control-related parameters (e.g., direction and strength of control) are customizable by shaping the reward.

My recent work includes a wide range of real-world problmes such as solving combinatorial optimization problem for daily diet planning [KDD'21, Neurips'21], proposing interactive recommender systems for indoor shopping [KDD'22], developing empathetic chatbot for the people in the need of counseling [Not yet], and designing edit plans for drug discovery [Not yet].

In summary, my research focus is primarily on bridging NLP techniques and RL frameworks without gaps and solving real-world problems with an interplay between them. However, this does not mean that my research interests are limited to that field.

I am currently Ph.D candidate at Ulsan National Institute of Science and Technology (UNIST) in South Korea, advised by Chiehyeon Lim. I received my Bachelor's degree in economics from Ajou University in 2015. During the Ph.D. course, I experienced an intership at Hyundai Mipo Dockyard as a data scientist and analyzed manufacturing process data. Also, as a research scholarship student, I collaborated with Daewoong Pharmaceutical to carry out a research project of drug discvoery. I will graduate from my PhD in February 2023 and am currently seeking a post-doc position. I am open to all suggestions, so please feel free to contact me.
